Welcome to monospace.com as it existed in early 2001

This page is a perfect artifact of the early-2000s web — a time when personal sites were handcrafted, proudly idiosyncratic, and deeply tied to the creator’s identity. I built it just as the dot-com bubble had burst and before social media centralized personal expression, capturing a moment when the web still felt like a frontier: chaotic, expressive, and full of experiments.

Every detail — the table-based layout, tiny pixel GIF spacers, JavaScript bookmarklets, and custom redirect logic — reflects my curiosity and hands-on tinkering within the limits of the browsers of the era: Netscape 4, Internet Explorer 5, and early Flash plug-ins. The content also shows my perspective and cultural touchpoints: personal commentary, curated links, RealMedia audio files, Shockwave games, hand-rolled “featured links,” and a sidebar showcasing some of my favorite personal projects.

This page embodies the pre-Web-2.0 ethos of the internet as a space for individuals, not platforms, when people built their own corners of the web as living rooms full of links, jokes, music, art, and personal obsessions. In that sense, it isn’t just nostalgic; it’s a time capsule from an era when being “online” meant creating your own world.
